As an IT Leadership intern for Thermo Fisher Scientific this summer, I expanded upon many software, data analytics, and leadership skills. After my first month at the company, I discovered an opportunity for Thermo Fisher to improve communication amongst employees across the company's many sites. The IT Team was being overloaded with Service Now requests while employees had to wait weeks before someone from IT could help them.

I designed and implemented a web-application in the Office 365 environment that allows employees to better leverage the diverse skill sets that exist within the company when they run into common issues. When employees have issues with using a certain software, their devices, or need consultation on specific areas of expertise (project management, for example), they can look employees up by name, location, or skill in this application. Once they find an employee they want to reach out, the applications connects the two and documents the requests employees needed assistance with. I am working to modify and polish this application to integrate PostgreSQL and Python to more efficiently document, access, and store large data. This application can help the company figure out how to move internal processes faster and pinpoint where common difficulties occur. The Director of IT would like me to continue building this application so that Thermo Fisher IT can implement it in the future.

In addition to this independent project, I co-led the IT Standard Processes Project with my manager. This project focused on restructuring processes within IT through standardization and aimed to better serve customers through a more unified internal approach. Collaborating with IT managers across Thermo Fisher's sites, I defined what data to collect to identify what to standardize. I then manipulated, extracted, and transformed the data using tools like PowerBI, SQL databases and SharpCloud to present the cleaned data in regular meetings.

As a software Intern with the Risk Management Team, my primary role was to work with the MIT Safari Project, a situational-awareness program written by MIT PhD candidates in Python. I learned how to work within an Ubuntu environment of a Linux PC and how to use Guake Terminal to launch the program and its many servers. Although a valuable tool, the project was out of use due to errors in code and changing/misplacing of file locations and folders and my task was to get it running again as the candidates who wrote it were no longer at MIT.

Over three months, I used the debugging console and expanded my knowledge of Python by trying to fix the several components of the project. I made use of Python documentation to address errors I had little knowledge of and wrote a guide that office members with little to no coding experience could use to launch the program and its servers.

In addition to this project, I also completed an independent project in which I implemented a K-means clustering tool for enhanced data analytics and presented it to upper management. I integrated it with Tableau, a visualization tool, to simplify k-means clustering for those I was presenting to and showed them how they could extend the functionality of simple tools by embedding code in them.

Currently, we are working on creating a tool that allows the Team Leads to record all new applicants' applications, interviews, and preferences for what projects they want to work on.

This semester, I am working on structuring and hosting the Team Website using a node.js framework. The current website relies primarily HTML and CSS, and internal tools rely on PHP. I hope to teach myself how to leverage node.js and other frameworks such as vue.js and express.js to create faster and more "light-weight" website and internal tools.
